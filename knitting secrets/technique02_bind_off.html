Bind Off

When you complete the last row of your pattern you will begin to bind off just as if you were continuing the pattern. If you are using a pattern without a definite end you would start casting off when you have knitted until you have at least enough yarn to make about three times the length of a row.

  1. Start by knitting or purling (or whatever pattern stitch you were working in) the first two stitches of the row.
  2. Once you have two stitches on the right-hand needle, use the tip of the left-hand needle to pull the first stitch back up and over the second stitch and over the tip of the right-hand needle. This will leave one stitch, actually the second stitch you worked, on the right-hand needle.
  3. Knit or purl the next stitch as needed and repeat the process until you are left with no stitches on the left-hand needle and one stitch on the right-hand needle.
  4. Slip this last stitch off the needle. Trim your working yarn so that you have a few inches left over to weave into the finished work.
  5. Weave the end of the yarn through several stitches to secure the end, using either a sewing needle or a crochet hook.
