So, you have decided to start knitting, huh? That is awesome! We, at ChiChi Squirrel, are very excited for you and are happy to virtually hold your hands while you are going through the first knitting steps!

You might not believe it at the moment, but, let me tell you, knitting is very EASY and it’s a lot of FUN. To start you only need some basic knitting tools, knitting material, and the step-by-step instructions.

Equipment and Materials

      Equipment / Tools

    • 1 pair of knitting needles
    • 1 pair of scissors

      Material

    • 1 ball of yarn

Before you start searching for those items, and get overwhelmed by the variety, let us clarify some basic characteristics of knitting needles and yarn.

Knitting needles

Types of knitting needles

  1. Straight - for any project
  2. Circular - for knitting in round or for holding a large amount of stitches; works great for hats, for example, or blankets!
  3. Double-pointed - for knitting in square, for example, for socks
Our advice

Start with the straight needles - easy to handle, suitable for almost any project, more affordable. Good choice all around.

Material of knitting needles

  1. bamboo
  2. metal
  3. plastic
Our advice

Choose what you like the most! Go to a local store and try and feel both types. Some people like the “soft” touch of bamboo, others prefer how smoothly yarn slides over the metal needles. So, we encourage you to find out what works for you!

Sizes of knitting needles

The size of knitting needles varies from 2 mm to 25mm.

Usually the size of knitting needles is defined by the project itself or the final result you are going for. For example, sizes 0-1 are perfect for laces, 1-3 for socks, 11-17 for a bulky blanket or a cardigan. Refer to a guide below to have a better understanding of knitting needle sizes!

Our advice

size 6, 7 or 8. Accurate knitting needle size is very important for any project. This will essentially determine how fine your stitches are going to be. If you just start, we recommend getting just 1 pair of knitting needles in size 6, 7, or 8. These are good medium options to get your hands and fingers used to knitting without putting too much tension on them.

Yarn

Yarns can go in different materials, textures, colors, and weights. All these details are usually displayed on the yarn’s label.

Yarn material, color and texture are pretty straightforward characteristics. The one that might be a bit confusing even for pros is yarn weight. Let’s break it down.

Yarn weight

Yarn weight refers to the thickness of the yarn. It's a range, from super fine to super bulky.

Below is a great chart that shows all the types of yarn weight, what projects that can be used for and what size of knitting needles work best for each.

Our advice

medium weight, any material or color, but make sure you LOVE it. It’s the most fun part - to be able to choose the color, style and design of your project. Besides, this will encourage you to keep going, and learn more and practise more.

Hope we haven’t bored you or, worse, scared you with the amount of information. It is much better to be aware of all the characteristics above before you start your first project. Right tools and materials are big parts of a successful project, so star forming your understanding of these fundamental concepts early on seems like a very good idea. And even if it doesn’t make much sense at the moment, don’t worry, it will click eventually.

With that, let's move on to the next step!

Cast On

All knitting starts with casting on. This creates loops on the needle which will become the first row of stitches.

There are many different methods for casting on, but we will start with the easiest - the single cast on.

As this is a technique, we have added it to the Knitting Secrets page, so you start getting used to using this handy library of tips and tricks! Head over to Cast on page and see a great guide for how to create a single cast on.

Knit

With all stitches being casted on, let’s start knitting!

For these first knits we will learn a garter stitch. What is the best place to learn new stitches and techniques around here? That’s right! It’s Knitting Secrets page. Follow the Garter Stitch tutorial from the library, and come back here for the final step!

Bind Off

You made it so far - great job!. This is the final step for every knitting piece - the bind off.

Binding off is a way of getting your project off your knitting needles. It is a simple and quick method for making a finished edge.

Follow the Bind Off tutorial from our Knitting Secrets page for this very final step.

Congratulations! You now know how to knit! From here you can just keep trying different techniques and stitches to expand your skills, or go straight to the project page and get your hands on some simple projects!

Good luck!
